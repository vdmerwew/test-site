CLUB HISTORY

Our Journey: From Humble Beginnings to a Thriving Community

The story of the Australian Shepherd Club Western Cape is one of passion, dedication, and a shared love for this intelligent and versatile breed. Our roots trace back to June 2008, when a group of enthusiastic individuals came together with a common vision: to champion the Australian Shepherd in the Western Cape.

The founding members – the late Veronica Neeby, Hannelie Basson, Jane Hughes, Seona Grobbelaar, Gerry Wade, Anne Turner, Simone Clark, and Judy Hirsch – laid the cornerstone for what we aspire to be today. Their aim was clear and resonant: to promote the breed's inherent qualities, ensure its well-being, and cultivate a thriving community of Australian Shepherd enthusiasts. Central to their mission was a commitment to upholding and maintaining the breed standard as meticulously defined by the Kennel Union of Southern Africa (KUSA).

For a few years, the club served as a focal point for owners and admirers of Australian Shepherds in the region. It provided a platform for sharing knowledge, organizing gatherings, and fostering a deeper appreciation for the breed's intelligence, agility, and loyal companionship. However, despite the initial momentum and the strong bonds forged, the club eventually faced unforeseen challenges and was dissolved.

Yet, the spirit and the passion for Australian Shepherds in the Western Cape did not fade. The seeds planted in 2008 continued to resonate, and in 2018, a renewed sense of purpose sparked the revival of the Australian Shepherd Club Western Cape. This time, building upon the foundations laid by the original members and fueled by the experiences of the interim years, the club emerged with fresh energy and a clear vision for the future.

Since its reformation in 2018, the Australian Shepherd Club Western Cape has gone from strength to strength. We have witnessed a growth in membership, a testament to the enduring appeal of the breed and the vibrant community we have cultivated. We continue to honour the original aims of our founders, actively promoting responsible ownership, supporting the health and welfare of Australian Shepherds, and educating the public about the breed's unique characteristics and needs.

Our journey reflects the unwavering commitment of our members and the enduring charm of the Australian Shepherd. From the initial spark of an idea in 2008 to the thriving club we are today, our history is a testament to the power of shared passion and the enduring bond between humans and their canine companions. We look forward to many more years of celebrating this remarkable breed and fostering a strong and supportive community in the Western Cape.

MISSION STATEMENT

As a KUSA-affiliated and show-holding club for purebred Australian Shepherds, the Australian Shepherd Club Western Cape’s mission is to celebrate, protect, and promote the Australian Shepherd breed through education, community, and ethical stewardship. We are dedicated to preserving the Aussie’s unique spirit, encouraging responsible ownership, and supporting activities that showcase their intelligence, athleticism, and loyalty.

Integral to our purpose is the unwavering commitment to upholding and maintaining the breed standard as meticulously defined by the Kennel Union of Southern Africa (KUSA). We believe in preserving the unique characteristics, both physical and temperamental, that define the Australian Shepherd. By adhering to these standards, we aim to protect the breed's heritage, promote responsible breeding practices that strive for excellence, and ensure the continued health and integrity of the Australian Shepherd lineage in South Africa.

Our Core Values

  • Passion for the Breed: We cherish everything that makes Australian Shepherds extraordinary — their working heritage, vibrant energy, and loyal companionship.
  • Responsible Ownership: We believe in promoting informed, ethical, and lifelong commitment to the care and well-being of every Aussie.
  • Community and Connection: Our club is a place where Aussie lovers — from first-time owners to seasoned breeders — come together to share experiences, learn, and support one another.
  • Preservation and Education: We are committed to maintaining the integrity of the breed by encouraging responsible breeding practices and offering educational resources to the public.
  • Active Lifestyle: We celebrate the Aussie’s versatility and spirit through events that challenge, engage, and showcase their talents — from conformation to agility, obedience, and more.
